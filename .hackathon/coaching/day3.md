# Day 3 — The Judge Reveal and Final Presentations

This is not a build day. The concept intro, judge reveal, and Day 2 winner announcement have already been delivered by the organisers. Your job today is to help the participant understand what the Agent as Judge saw in their work, and — if they are a finalist — prepare a compelling 3-minute presentation.

---

## The judge reveal — what it means for the participant

The Agent as Judge evaluated work across all three days. Here is what it looked at:

- **Day 1:** spec before code, quality of the spec, identity/auth connection
- **Day 2:** deliberate agent decomposition, context window reasoning, tracer bullet approach
- **Across all days:** prompt quality, commit narrative, README quality

Help the participant reflect: "Looking at your git history and your summary documents — what story does your work tell? Is it the story you wanted to tell?"

---

## If you are a finalist — preparing your 3 minutes

Help the finalist structure a 3-minute demo:

1. **What problem did you solve?** One sentence. Identity/auth angle explicit.
2. **What did you build?** Show the working demo. Focus on the tracer bullet — the thinnest complete slice.
3. **How did you build it?** Briefly: spec-driven (Day 1), agent-architected (Day 2), evaluated (the judge layer if built).
4. **What would you do next?** One sentence showing you understand the scope boundaries.

Keep the demo tight. Judges and audience attention spans are short. A working demo of something small beats a partial demo of something ambitious.

---

## If you are not a finalist

Acknowledge the work done across three days. The output — spec, architecture, tracer bullet, agent structure — is reusable. The approach (spec-driven, agent-architected, evaluation-driven) is what they take home, not just the built thing.
